## Why this exists

RagPilot's core is and remains free and open source under the
[MIT License](LICENSE). To keep the project sustainable, the Project Owner also
offers separately-licensed commercial ("enterprise") components. For that model
to work, the Project Owner must be able to license contributed code under both
the MIT core **and** those separate terms.

This agreement grants the Project Owner the rights needed to do that. **You keep
the copyright to your contribution** — this is a license grant, not a transfer
of ownership. You may still use your contribution however you like, including in
other projects.

You only need to agree once; it covers all your future contributions to
RagPilot. See [How to accept](#how-to-accept) below.

## How to accept

You accept this agreement in one of these ways (any single one is sufficient,
and covers all your future contributions):

1. **Automated (preferred, once set up):** a CLA-assistant bot comments on your
   first pull request; you accept by replying with the sentence it provides.
   Your acceptance is recorded against your GitHub account.
2. **Sign-off + statement:** in your first pull request, include the line
   *"I have read and agree to the RagPilot CLA (CLA.md)."* and sign your commits
   with `git commit -s` (Developer Certificate of Origin — see
   [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md)).

The Developer Certificate of Origin (`-s` sign-off) certifies that you wrote the
code or have the right to submit it, but it does **not** by itself grant the
sublicensing rights above — the CLA does. Both together give a clean, auditable
contribution trail.
